Imposter syndrome is definetly something every artist has experienced at some point. Personally, I believe that in a certain stage, that problem becomes bigger the further ones skills progress.
While I haven't found a cure for this phenomenon yet, I'll share some ideas and short term solutions that I've found.

## 1. It will probably get you at some point ##
As long as you aren't a person of extreme confidence and a crazy extrovert, you will more likly than not face this feeling at some point.

## 2. It's not a permanent thing ##
The feelings of imposter syndrome come and go. One of my theories is, that it has a lot to do with the overall mood and the level of happiness that influences how you feel about yourself and what you made.

## 3. There are things that you can do ##
While there probably isn't a way to completely shield yourself from imposter syndrome, you can indeed do certain things to make it easier to deal with. 

# Practical Techniques
One of the biggest things you can actually do is asking other people for critique. Those should idealy be at least on more or less the same skill level as you, though a higher skill level doesn't hurt. Also, if you do post your artwork on the internet, do so either way. Even when your artwork isn't as good as usual, there will always be people who appriciate it. Always.
Another great way is to look at older artwork. Maybe from a month ago, maybe from a year ago. This does require you to actually save and keep your artwork though, which is another thing I'd like you to greatly advise.
If you feel the need to draw but are discuraged by negative thoughts after just a few seconds, just keep at it. Not every work will be perfect. Every artist, without exclusion has made at least a few dozens of "bad work" by the time they became great.

# My experience #
I have had many experiences with the topic of imposter syndrome. Creating this site, as specially talking and researching the topic for this post has greatly changed my perspective and feelings about it. The thing that helped me the most, was probably sharing my work with a good friend, who always managed to get my mood up a bit. 

So remember, these negative feelings aren't made of facts, but just by your mind. Take care guys-
