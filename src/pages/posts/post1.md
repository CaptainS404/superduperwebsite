Congrats! You reached my first post. Here, I will talk a bit about my setup, which I use for my daily work, art and school.
My main work happens either on my desktop PC or my laptop, while I use the first for digital art. I'll get into the specifics later.

Main topics of this post:
-my philosophy with technology
-my PC/laptop
-specific software that I use
-my experience with Linux

I'm a huge open-source enthusiast, which is why I use Linux for pretty much anything and on any device. The only reason I still have a
Windows 11 partition on my main PC is that I still need some programs like Autodesk Inventor for school, also I just like to use Clip Studio Paint 
every now and then. I generally don't like software which I have no control or knowledge over what they phone home or do with my PC. That's also my 
main problem with Windows, and why I don't really like having it on my PC.

## For my mainly used devices ##

My laptop.. 
Once upon a time, (2-3 months to be exact), I bought a used Lenovo Thinkpad T470s for about 160$ on eBay. It has only one battery, which means it oly 
has about 1h of battery time. 
It has the following components, for those interested:
- **Intel Core i5 7330U**
- **16GB DDR4 RAM**
- **256 SSD**
- **touchscreen**

Sure, it's getting a little old, does no longer support Windows 11, with Linux though, it's still an absolute beast.
I haven't tried that many distros for now, Ubuntu 24 has worked great though, more on that later. I don't use it much for any intensive tasks, I mostly 
just use it for FreeCAD and writing.

My Desktop PC..
That's where most of my harder work takes place. I use it for my digital art, 3D modelling/rendering and, of course, some occasional gaming.
Everything started off with an old HP-Workstation, since I didn't have any money. I upgraded part after part, until basically everything was upgraded.
My components:
- **AMD Ryzen 5 5600**
- **16GB DDR4**
- **RTX 3060 12GB**
- **512GB NVMe SSD**
- **1TB + 2TB Crucial 2.5' SSD**

It's fast enough for my uses, the components cost about 700-800$ in total at the time I bought the parts.

## Now for Ubuntu.. ##
Well, in my opinion, it is indeed an easy to use distro. **However**, it has brought some problems over the time. 
My first bad experience with it was a broken installer, while I was originally installing Ubuntu on my laptop, the installer
randomly broke and I had to restart the installation. Other slight problems I had were about snaps, I just don't like them.
Snaps tend to just not work here and there, **I just don't like them v2**.
With CachyOS, which is my main OS on my desktop PC, I haven't really had any problems at all, all my drivers, programs and games just work 
and have worked out of box.

For now, I got everything to work how I want it and don't plan of changing anything.
